  • Fitbit

    Fitbit is known for its products of the same name, which are activity trackers, wireless-enabled wearable technology devices that measure data such as the number of steps walked, quality of sleep, steps climbed, and other personal metrics. The first of these was the Fitbit Tracker.

  • Apple

    Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California, that designs, develops, and sells consumer electronics, computer software, and online services. It launched its first wearable device Apple Watch on September 9, 2014 and imediately claimed a major stake in the wearable industry. Market experts estimate that Apple will eventually take the number one position as a wearable technology vendor.

  • Xiaomi

    The Chinese company Xiaomi Inc. is one of the largest smartphone manufacturers in the world. Xiaomi designs, develops, and sells smartphones, mobile apps, and related consumer electronics. Their wearable fitness monitor and sleep tracker Mi Band was announced in August 2014 and it is one of the most sold fitness trackers in the world.

  • Garmin

    Garmin develops consumer, aviation, outdoor, fitness, and marine technologies for the Global Positioning System. Garmin Ltd. is the Swiss parent company of a group of companies founded in 1989 by Gary Burrell and Min Kao. Garmin manufactures a myriad of different smart wearables. For example the Vivoactive has GPS to accurately track running, cycling and swimming with live pace and distances.

  • Samsung

    Samsung Electronics Ltd. is a South Korean multinational electronics company headquartered in Suwon, South Korea. Like Apple they manufacture smartwatches that are easy to use with their smartphones. This company has a lot of potential in the wearables industry.
